Course Objectives

By the end of the course you will be able to do the following.

  1. Identify and explain key concepts from feminist theory approaches, intersectionality, and critical making as they apply to the AI tools and texts covered and worked within the course.
  2. Analyze how generative AI represents identity in its outputs in the areas of race, gender, class, language, and other categories, and evaluate how these representations shape what gets to be determined as legitimate and legible.
  3. Assess and critique how generative outputs reflect epistemologies that remove intersectional experiences.
  4. Interrogate AI-generated artifacts, textually and visually, as rhetorically malleable constructs determined by data and design choices, and not as neutral information.
  5. Regularly generate and document interactions with AI tools through a prompt journal.
  6. Design and build a small research project that audits AI tools from a feminist standpoint through a chosen identity marker.
