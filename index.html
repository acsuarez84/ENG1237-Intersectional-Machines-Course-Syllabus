language

What happens to dialect, register, and multilingual voice when generative AI rewrites it.

Theories & authors: Slator, “He Said, She Said” (gender in machine translation); Noble, “Google Search”; Crenshaw on intersectional voice.

geography

Where AI is built, whose data it harvests, and which places it centers or pushes to the margins.

Theories & authors: West et al., “Discriminating Systems”; Crawford & Paglen, “Excavating AI.”

education

How AI enters classrooms and the digital humanities, and whom its tools include or leave out.

Theories & authors: Kirschenbaum, “What is DH?”; Williams, “Disability, Universal Design & the DH”; Ratto, “Critical Making.”

body

How AI reads — and mis-reads — bodies, from facial recognition to medical risk.

Theories & authors: Buolamwini on the “coded gaze”; Williams on universal design; the healthcare risk-algorithm case (Obermeyer).

political

AI in law, policing, and public benefits — and whom it disadvantages.

Theories & authors: Crenshaw on antidiscrimination law; Angwin et al., “Machine Bias” (COMPAS); the MIDAS and Idaho Medicaid cases.

socioeconomic

AI gatekeeping jobs, credit, and housing.

Theories & authors: Amazon’s hiring tool; the Apple Card and mortgage-approval cases; West, “Redistribution and Rekognition.”

class

Who holds power in the AI industry and whose labor is buried in the data.

Theories & authors: West et al., “Discriminating Systems” (the “diversity crisis”); Wajcman, “Feminist Theories of Technology.”

representation

How AI pictures — and erases — identity in text and image.

Theories & authors: Crawford & Paglen, “Excavating AI”; Noble on hyper-visibility / invisibility; the Stable Diffusion and Gemini image cases.
