Assignment Overview, Grading & Late Policy

Assignments, point values, the grading scale, and late work.

This course has five (5) low-stakes, frequent assignments and two (2) high-stakes assignments. The low-stakes assignments are created to build habits of regular engagement and are not meant to be perfect but rather a development of critical analysis and how the process builds your argument. They are low-stakes because of the point values, but they are all part of the pieces leading to your final artifact submission. The high-stakes assignments are where the low-stakes assignments accumulate to structure your findings and artifact design (AI or analog), all as an embodied argument.

Late Submissions

I understand that things happen outside of our control. Hence, for all assignments, there is a 1-week grace period for due work. Please keep in mind that towards the end of the semester, I must submit grades by a specific date; therefore, all assignments must be submitted by that date (specific semester date TBD).

Participation Grade

Participation is graded through your weekly reflections and peer responses. Do not miss these assignments because the accumulation of lost points can affect your grade. Plus, engaging in these weekly written and class discussions will build your critical analysis of AI.
