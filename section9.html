Class-by-Class Schedule

The full week-by-week plan (based on the 2028 Fall calendar). Mondays are readings & discussion, Wednesdays are Theory Lab, Fridays are Workshop. All assignments are submitted via Web Courses.

How the week works: Mondays — readings & class discussions. Wednesdays — learning AI and analog designs (Theory Lab). Fridays — Workshop: building your journal entries. Click any week to expand it.

1Week 1: Kirschenbaum & DH foundationsAug 28 – Sep 1

8/28 · Assigned Reading

Kirschenbaum, “What is Digital Humanities and What’s It Doing in English Departments?”

8/30 · Discussion & Concept Mapping

Kirschenbaum. Small groups sketch a visual diagram of what “digital humanities” is as per Kirschenbaum’s argument.

9/1 · Workshop 1

Setting up your journal; introduce the AI tools listed in the syllabus; go over the case studies list for non-users. Next week’s reading: Svensson.

Readings & Focus Points

  • Argument: What do you think defines digital humanities as a field? Do you agree or disagree with Kirschenbaum’s argument?
  • Key Terms: How does Kirschenbaum use the term “humanities computing,” and what do you make of the shift into “digital humanities” (DH) he traces?
  • Connection: Based on the reading, how would you argue AI tools should or should not be understood as part of DH’s toolkit?

Assignments Due via Web Courses

Syllabus acknowledgment & Attendance verification; Kirschenbaum Discussion post — Due 9/1/28, 11:59pm
2Week 2: Svensson & the naming of DHSep 4 – Sep 8

9/4 · Labor Day — No Class

Svensson discussion moved to 9/6.

9/6 · Discussion & Theory Lab (compressed)

Svensson. Students apply the “humanities computing to DH” shift to a textual map AI example using Claude AI. Next week’s reading: Crenshaw.

9/8 · Workshop 2

Journal Entry: work with AI / case studies.

Readings & Focus Points

  • Argument: What does Svensson suggest happened when the field of DH renamed itself? Do you see that as a meaningful change or just a rebrand?
  • Key Terms: How does Svensson characterize the institutional/discursive change, and what questions does that raise for you?
  • Connection: What do you think a field’s self-renaming reveals about how it will approach new technologies like generative AI?

Assignments Due via Web Courses

Svensson Discussion post — Due 9/8/28, 11:59pm
3Week 3: Crenshaw & intersectionalitySep 11 – Sep 15

9/11 · Discussion

Crenshaw, “Demarginalizing the Intersection of Race and Sex.”

9/13 · Theory Lab — Close Reading of AI

As a class, students annotate a real AI output on screen for single-axis versus intersectionality from Claude and ChatGPT. Next week’s reading: SEP “Feminist Philosophy.”

9/15 · Workshop 3

Journal Entry: work with AI / case studies; build Claude’s textual map using already-recorded prompt outputs from earlier weeks.

Readings & Focus Points

  • Argument: What problem does Crenshaw identify with single-axis approaches to discrimination law, and how convincing do you find her case?
  • Key Terms: How would you explain intersectionality in your own words, based on this piece?
  • Connection: Where do you see AI systems making similar single-axis decisions?

Assignments Due via Web Courses

Claude Artifact — Textual Map — Due 9/15/28, 11:59pm
4Week 4: Feminist Philosophy (SEP)Sep 18 – Sep 22

9/18 · Discussion

SEP, “Feminist Philosophy.”

9/20 · Theory Lab — Jigsaw Teaching

In small groups, students present one prompt and their outputs, using 3 of the feminist branches—feminist standpoint theory, intersectional feminism, and postmodern/poststructuralist feminism—to analyze and critique it. Then each student develops a new prompt based on this exercise in connection to the theory.

9/22 · Workshop 4

Journal Entry: work with AI / case studies. No new reading assigned for week 5—it will be dedicated as a workshop week.

Readings & Focus Points

  • Argument: Focusing on the three branches this week, what values do you see them sharing despite their differences? Where do you think their tensions mostly lie?
  • Key Terms: Which concept do you find most useful and why, in connection to the argument?
  • Connection: Which framework here would you choose to critique AI, and how would you apply it?

Assignments Due via Web Courses

SEP Discussion post — Due 9/22/28, 11:59pm
