Class-by-Class Schedule

The full week-by-week plan (based on the 2028 Fall calendar). Mondays are readings & discussion, Wednesdays are Theory Lab, Fridays are Workshop. All assignments are submitted via Web Courses.

8/21 · Assigned Reading

Kirschenbaum, “What is Digital Humanities and What’s It Doing in English Departments?”

  • Argument: What do you think defines digital humanities as a field? Do you agree or disagree with Kirschenbaum’s argument?
  • Key Terms: How does Kirschenbaum use the term “humanities computing,” and what do you make of the shift into “digital humanities” (DH) he traces?
  • Connection: Based on the reading, how would you argue AI tools should or should not be understood as part of DH’s toolkit?

9/6 · Discussion & Theory Lab (compressed)

Crenshaw, “Demarginalizing the Intersection of Race and Sex.”

  • Argument: What problem does Crenshaw identify with single-axis approaches to discrimination law, and how convincing do you find her case?

  • Connection: Where do you see, or not, AI systems making similar single-axis decisions?

9/11 · Discussion

SEP, “Feminist Philosophy.”

  • Argument: Focusing on the three branches this week, what values do you see the 3 branches sharing despite their differences? Where do you think their tensions mostly lie?
  • Key Terms: Which concept do you find most useful and why, in connection to the argument?
  • Connection: Which framework here would you choose to critique AI, and how would you apply it?

9/13 · Theory Lab — Jigsaw Teaching

In small groups, students present one prompt and their outputs, using 3 of the feminist branches:

  1. feminist standpoint theory,
  2. intersectional feminism, and
  3. postmodern/poststructuralist feminism

to analyze and critique it. Then each student develops a new prompt based on this exercise in connection to the theory.
